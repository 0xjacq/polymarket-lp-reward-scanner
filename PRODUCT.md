# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Primary users are active Polymarket LP operators, quant-leaning traders, and power users who monitor reward opportunities multiple times per day. They usually work in high-focus sessions, often with many tabs and external tools open, and need to scan quickly under time pressure before market conditions change.

## Product Purpose
Napolyrewardfarmor exists to surface the best LP reward opportunities from snapshot data, then let users validate execution context with live detail (orderbook, queue, spread, price history) before placing quotes. Success means faster decision cycles, reduced cognitive overhead during scanning, and fewer missed opportunities due to unclear ranking or weak information hierarchy.

## Brand Personality
Precise, alert, and disciplined. The interface should feel like an operator console: trustworthy metrics, strong signal-to-noise ratio, and predictable interaction behavior. Tone stays factual and concise, with no hype language.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS card galleries with repetitive icon-heading-description blocks.
- Decorative glassmorphism and blurred floating panes used as visual filler.
- Neon-heavy crypto dashboards with noisy gradients and low information clarity.
- Marketing-style hero metrics that reduce actionable context.

## Design Principles
1. Rank signal first: put actionable ranking and decision metrics at the center of visual hierarchy.
2. Fast scan beats decoration: every visual element must improve comprehension speed.
3. Stable interaction rhythm: filters, sorting, and details should behave consistently and predictably.
4. Live context without chaos: real-time updates must be legible without visual jitter.
5. Dense but humane: preserve information density while keeping spacing, typography, and state feedback clear.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA for contrast and keyboard navigation. Ensure visible focus states, clear labels for controls, resilient behavior at 390px width, and touch targets sized for mobile use where interactive controls appear. Respect reduced motion by avoiding motion patterns that can disorient users.
